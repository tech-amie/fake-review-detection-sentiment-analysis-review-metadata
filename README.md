The advent of internet shopping and online purchasing means that the importance of consumer reviews has increased significantly. Fake reviews pose a great threat to consumers and adversely affect fair business practices. As such the identification of fake reviews is increasingly important. A recent survey by brightlocal  (2026) found that star ratings and review recency are more important than ever before and that consumers are demanding accountability for fake reviews. The objective of this project is to investigate the current research and data science techniques that are used to identify fake reviews and what consumers can do to protect themselves. Another approach (PNAS 2022) suggests that it is more effective to determine which products have purchased fake reviews and this is highly clustered due to the products reliance on a common set of reviewers.
The purpose of this project was to develop a notebook to perfrom EDA on the YELP labeled dataset, create new fetures for analysis using various ML classifiers.
